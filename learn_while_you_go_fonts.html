Module 2 · 30 min · Beginner

Building your first Claude prompt from scratch

By the end of this module, you'll write a prompt that actually works. Not a generic template. Your prompt. For your real work.

Why prompts fail

Most people write prompts like they're texting a friend. Vague. Assuming context. Hoping Claude figures it out. It won't. Claude is literal. Give it ambiguity, and it defaults to generic.

The golden rule: Context always beats cleverness.

Three elements of a working prompt

1. Role

Who are you asking Claude to be? "You are a brand strategist" is different from "You are a marketing copywriter."

2. Context

What does Claude need to know? Your industry. Your audience. Your brand voice. The more specific, the better.

3. Output format

What do you want back? A list. A paragraph. Bullet points. JSON. Be explicit.
